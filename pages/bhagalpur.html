About Bhagalpur

Bhagalpur is a major city in Bihar, historically connected with trade, education, river culture, and the silk industry. For me, it is also the place where local relationships, responsibility, and public awareness began.

Learn more: Wikipedia

City Highlights

Silk Industry

Bhagalpur is widely known for silk weaving and premium Tussar silk production.

Vikramshila

An ancient center of Buddhist learning and one of the region's major heritage symbols.

River Ganga

The Ganga shapes culture, agriculture, movement, and the emotional life of the city.

Population & Growth

A growing urban center of eastern Bihar with expanding education, trade, and services.

Geography & River Ganga

The River Ganga flows beside Bhagalpur, shaping its agriculture, trade, and spiritual culture.

Culture & Festivals

The city celebrates Chhath Puja, Durga Puja, Eid, and various cultural fairs. Bhagalpur blends traditional Bihar culture with modern urban life.

Education & Institutions

Bhagalpur is home to Tilka Manjhi Bhagalpur University (TMBU), established in 1960 — one of the major universities in eastern Bihar. The city has numerous schools, colleges, and professional institutions serving students across the region and neighboring districts.

Economy & Trade

Beyond silk, Bhagalpur's economy is driven by agriculture, small-scale industries, and a growing trade sector. The city is a regional commercial hub in eastern Bihar, with improving road and rail connectivity attracting new business activity and investment.

Connectivity

Rail

Bhagalpur Junction is a major railway station connecting the city to Kolkata, Delhi, and beyond.

Road

Connected via NH-80 and NH-131 with expanding inter-city and state highway infrastructure.

River

The sacred Ganga provides natural connectivity and supports local trade, transport, and agriculture.
