Pillar 1 — Learn

Understand digital currency from the ground up.

Before using or building anything, the community starts here — simple, honest explanations of what a digital coin is, how it differs from cash, why identity matters, and how UPI-style familiarity can make digital money accessible to everyone.

🪙

What is a digital coin?

A digital coin is a unit of value recorded on a ledger — like a number in a secure digital notebook — that people can earn, hold, and use according to transparent rules.

🏦

How does UPI connect?

UPI taught India to pay by scanning and confirming. A digital coin uses the same mental model: verify identity, approve transaction, receive proof — no confusion.

🪪

Why identity comes first

A coin without identity is just a number. HI Coin ties every wallet to a Human Digital Identity (HDI) so balances and records are personally accountable and meaningful.

🔒

Security basics

Your wallet lives in your browser's local storage, protected by device fingerprint and session keys. HI Vault encrypts the whole backup with AES-GCM. No server holds your keys.

📊

Supply rules matter

The prototype caps earnings at 99 HI per transaction to model responsible supply discipline. Real-world digital currencies need transparent, auditable issuance rules.

🤝

Community not speculation

This is not a trading platform. HI Coin is an ecosystem demo: earn through contribution, use for services, build trust through ledger history — value through real participation.

Pillar 2 — Understand

How it works under the hood.

The technology is not magic — it's a set of rules for recording, verifying, and protecting value. Understanding the stack helps you make better decisions as a user, builder, or policy contributor.

Technology Layer

Think like Bitcoin, design for everyday India.

Bitcoin proved that a public ledger, scarcity rules, and wallet ownership can create a new digital asset class. This concept adapts those principles for an India-first experience: identity-aware wallets, UPI-style usability, merchant records, and community trust — not speculation.

Ledger proof

Every coin movement produces a timestamped, inspectable record — auditable by the user without any third party.

Wallet ownership

Users need a clear wallet address, balance, history, and recovery path before any system can feel truly trustworthy.

Supply discipline

A serious coin model needs transparent rules for generation, earning limits, and visible total supply accounting.

Architecture

Four layers that make it real.

A professional digital currency connects coin issuance, wallet logic, UPI bridge thinking, and settlement rails into one coherent, auditable system.

Asset

Coin rules, token identity, and visible supply accounting.

Ledger

Transaction history, timestamps, and a complete audit trail.

Wallet

Address, balance, keys, identity binding, and permissions.

Rail

UPI-inspired payment UX and future merchant settlement records.
